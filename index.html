Education in India: British Raj to Modern Day

Click a year to see what changed.

1857

First Modern Universities

Universities were set up in Calcutta, Bombay and Madras. They followed a British academic model and mostly trained people for administrative jobs.

1882

Hunter Commission

The British government reviewed how education was working in India and discussed expanding primary schooling.

1904

Indian Universities Act

The colonial government increased control over universities and how they were managed.

1937

Wardha Education Plan

Gandhi proposed an education system where students would learn practical skills alongside academics.

1947

Independence

India gained independence and the government started shaping its own education policies.

1968

National Education Policy

India created a national policy to expand access to schools and improve the quality of education.

2009

Right to Education Act

Education became free and compulsory for children between ages 6 and 14.

2020

New Education Policy

Modern reforms introduced flexible subjects, skill learning and interdisciplinary education.
